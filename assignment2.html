Nielsen's Usability Heuristics analysis

Usability Heuristic Issues
Visibility of System Status It is easy to see how the system is moving through use of an appropriate moving icon on the tab that is currently being accessed.
Match Between the System and the Real World Terms, concepts, icons and images on The Tower website are easy to understand and are used appropriately throughout the website.
User Control and Freedom A user can see not only use the links across the top of the page that will take them to another section of the page but can also use the back button on the browser to return to a previous page and/or click on the title “The Tower” at the top of the page to return to the home page. There is also a header that pops down when a user leaves the home page that allows for easy access to other pages such as “News”, “Arts & Entertainment”, and “Politics” on each page allowing a user to easily navigate between subject areas.
Consistency and Standards The Tower website uses easily understandable language and formatting throughout the site similar to major news agency websites.
Error Prevention When searching a term that did not produce any results, The Tower website clearly states, “Nothing Found” and suggests trying different keywords for the search. There is no Advanced Search option when it comes to searching the documents within the webpage.
Recognition Rather than Recall The Tower website uses designs that are seen on other major news agency websites, lending rememberability to the functions of the different parts of the site. It uses site design techniques such as drop-down menus and pop down menus to find more information as one travels through the site.
Flexibility and Efficiency of Use The website for The Tower offers not only a search bar to directly search for an article about a topic of interest, but it also offers archives listed by date down the side of the page and again at the bottom of the page. The archives is unfortunately listed twice on each page, once on the left, once on the right, making the pages rather long to go through to reach the information at the bottom. It also offers a list of the most recent articles along the bottom of the page. The main page offers a scrolling bar of the most recent stories for ease of access. At the bottom of each page there is a “Return to top” link that returns the user to the top of the page which given the length of the archives listed on each page make it much easier to return to the top of the page.
Aesthetic and Minimalist Design The main page of The Tower offers approximately the last week’s worth of articles with pictures and a brief description of each article. They all have a “read more” button to easily access the article. It feels a bit busy with the size of the pictures and how many articles there are on the main page. If they were separated a little more into categories it may give a better flow. Within specific pages such as “News”, “Arts & Entertainment”, and “Politics” the articles are lined along with the page with no pictures, just titles and a brief description.
Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors When a user searches and nothing is found, there is a clear “Nothing Found” in large letters with a suggestion for possibly using different search keywords for better results.
Help and Documentation Aside from the prompt within the search when nothing is found, there does not appear to be any help documentation to help a user navigate around The Tower website.

Step 3: Summarize Part 1 with the top two strengths and top two weaknesses of the system.

Weakness 1:

Length of page – Having the archives listed twice on the page (once on the right and then once on the left) makes the webpage twice as long as it needs to be which can keep a person from going all the way to the bottom to find more information as needed.

Weakness 2:

No Advanced search option – having an advanced search option would help a user to focus their search more to weed out unnecessary items that pop up in the search. It would also offer the ability to find a particular article faster if it is not within the first screen of the results field.

Strength 1:

The Tower website offers an interactive pop-down menu that follows the user down the page allowing the user to switch between sections of the site quickly and easily.

Strength 2:

The Tower is up-to-date and is regularly updated allowing students access to news and information relevant quickly and easily.

Step 5: Brief comparison of the results of Part 1 and 2.

During the mini-usability test, it was clear (through non-verbal cues) the participant was frustrated with the search feature of the site. When questioned about the frustration, the participant responded that the lack of an advanced search feature limited the results he felt he could get on The Tower website. He felt the articles he did find were limited in their usefulness for a student looking for information on World War II. He stated that while he could find several articles on World War II he found significantly fewer articles on the Vietnam War and didn’t feel that he found any that gave campus perspectives of either conflict. The participant did say that he found the site appealing with the images that were available but that it could be improved by adding a drop-down menu or advanced search feature to be able to limit the search.

In conducting my own research on The Tower site, I found I had similar thoughts to the participant in that an advanced search would offer more fulfilling results. The search keyword I used was “smoking” and it resulted in two pages of results, of which at least two articles were about European laws about smoking. When I tried to limit my search to “smoking on campus” the search found no results so I tried “smoking” and “campus” which found several results about the banning of smoking on the campus of Catholic University, including an editorial disagreeing with the decision.

Overall, The Tower website is a fairly simple to use website that will be able to offer someone information quickly and easily but it could benefit from adding an advanced search option to be able to narrow down search results. One change that could visually be made is by removing one of the archives that are listed on the page, they make the page scroll too long and many people wouldn’t go to the bottom and may miss information that is useful.
